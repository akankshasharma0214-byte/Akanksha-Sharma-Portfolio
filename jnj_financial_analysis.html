Section 03
Trend Analysis

Revenue Trajectory — Transformation Creates Temporary Distortion. J&J's headline revenue shows apparent decline from $93.8B (FY2021–22) to $85.2B (FY2023) — but this is almost entirely explained by the Kenvue consumer health spin-off, which removed approximately $15B in annual revenue. On a continuing operations basis, the underlying Innovative Medicine and MedTech segments have grown consistently at 5–8% annually. The $88.8B FY2024 revenue represents strong underlying growth driven by DARZALEX crossing $11B in annual sales, Shockwave Medical integration, and MedTech procedure volume recovery. The forward revenue trajectory excluding DePuy Synthes (pending spinoff) will show another revenue step-down — but this is value-enhancing portfolio simplification, not deterioration.

Adj. EPS Pressure — Stelara LOE is the Central FY2025 Challenge. STELARA generated approximately $10.9B in FY2023 revenue and faces US biosimilar entry beginning January 2025 — creating a $3–4B annual revenue headwind as market share erodes over 2025–2026. Management's FY2025 Adjusted EPS guidance of $10.75–$10.95 implies only ~8% growth despite this significant headwind — demonstrating the offsetting power of DARZALEX expansion, CARVYKTI growth, and new launches. R&D investment has grown steadily from $12.2B to $16.4B over the study period — 18.5% of revenue in FY2024 — reflecting management's confidence in the pipeline filling the Stelara gap through FY2027–2028.

Cash Flow Quality — Exceptional and Consistent. J&J generates approximately $18–22B in free cash flow annually — one of the most consistent FCF generators in global healthcare. The AAA credit rating (one of only two US companies) reflects this financial strength. The dividend has been raised for 62 consecutive years — compounding at approximately 5–6% annually — making J&J a Dividend King. Capital allocation priorities have been acquisitions (Abiomed $16.6B FY2022, Shockwave $13.1B FY2024, Intra-Cellular FY2025) supplemented by steady buybacks. The FY2025 FCF of $20B+ is a record, signalling that Kenvue removal has improved the overall FCF quality of the remaining business.

Portfolio Sharpening — The New J&J Thesis. CEO Joaquin Duato has systematically sharpened J&J's focus to six high-growth, high-margin areas: Oncology, Immunology, Neuroscience, Cardiovascular, Surgery, and Vision. The Kenvue spin (2023), planned DePuy Synthes orthopaedics spin (2026–2027), and aggressive acquisition of innovation assets in cardiovascular (Shockwave, V-Wave) and neuroscience (Intra-Cellular) collectively represent a once-in-a-generation portfolio transformation. The financial implication is higher average revenue growth rates, higher operating margins, and re-rating potential from specialty pharma/medtech peers.

At approximately $165 per share, J&J trades at only 15.5x forward adjusted earnings — a meaningful discount to its 5-year historical average of ~18x and a significant discount to large-cap pharma peers. This "litigation discount" — applied by institutional investors due to ongoing talc-related lawsuits — appears excessive relative to the company's fundamental strength: AAA credit rating, $20B+ annual FCF, 62-year dividend growth streak, and a pipeline arguably stronger than at any point in recent history. Our DCF analysis at a 7.5% WACC and 3% terminal growth rate produces a base case intrinsic value of $170–210 per share — suggesting 10–25% upside from current levels. The primary catalyst for re-rating is resolution of the talc litigation — which would remove the institutional discount and allow the stock to trade in line with pharma peers at 18–20x earnings.

Section 08
FP&A Analyst — Executive Takeaway
For the CFO / Board of Directors

Johnson & Johnson is executing one of the most ambitious portfolio transformations in healthcare history — methodically divesting lower-growth assets (Kenvue 2023, DePuy Synthes 2026–2027) while simultaneously acquiring high-growth innovation assets in cardiovascular, neuroscience, and oncology. The financial profile of the "New J&J" — pure-play Innovative Medicine and high-value MedTech — will carry structurally higher operating margins, faster revenue growth, and superior capital efficiency than the historical conglomerate. The near-term financial narrative for the Board is dominated by the Stelara LOE transition in 2025–2026: management must demonstrate that DARZALEX expansion, CARVYKTI scale, RYBREVANT launch, and the neuroscience portfolio collectively generate enough growth to offset $3–4B in annual Stelara revenue erosion. FY2025 Adj. EPS guidance of $10.75–$10.95 (+8.7% growth) suggests management is confident — but execution against that guidance is the critical FY2025 test. The talc litigation resolution remains the single most important non-operating variable: a settlement would remove the institutional discount and potentially unlock 15–20% share price appreciation independent of operating performance.
