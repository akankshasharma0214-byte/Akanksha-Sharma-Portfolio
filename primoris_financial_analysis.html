Company Snapshot
A
Akanksha Sharma
FP&A Analyst · MS Financial Analytics, Stevens Institute of Technology

Primoris Services Corporation is a leading specialty contractor and infrastructure services company headquartered in Dallas, Texas. The company operates through two primary segments — Utilities (electric power, gas distribution, telecommunications) and Energy (renewables, industrial, pipeline) — serving utility companies, energy producers, and government entities across the United States and Canada.

Revenue is generated through a project-based model combining fixed-price, unit-price, and time-and-materials contracts, supplemented by long-term Master Service Agreements (MSAs) that provide recurring, predictable revenue streams. Primoris has grown aggressively through acquisition — including Future Infrastructure (2021), PLH Group and B Comm (2022–2023) — establishing itself as a critical partner in America's infrastructure renewal and energy transition buildout.

For FP&A analysts, Primoris is a benchmark company for understanding project accounting, backlog-driven revenue forecasting, CapEx allocation, and margin management in capital-intensive services businesses — directly comparable to PE-backed infrastructure platforms like Azuria Water Solutions.

Section 03
Trend Analysis

Revenue Growth — Infrastructure Supercycle Driving Exceptional Expansion. Primoris has grown revenue from $3.5B in 2020 to $6.4B in 2024 — an 82% increase over four years. Growth has been driven by two compounding forces: organic expansion in renewable energy construction and utility grid modernisation, and strategic acquisitions including Future Infrastructure Holdings ($622M, 2021), PLH Group, and B Comm in 2022-2023. The 2023 backlog inflection to $10.9B — nearly doubling year-over-year — signals a step-change in the company's revenue visibility and scale, with $11.9B in backlog as of year-end 2024 providing approximately 1.9 years of forward revenue coverage.

Margin Dynamics — Compressed Then Recovering. Gross margins compressed from 11.9% in 2020-2021 to 10.3% in 2022-2023 as the company absorbed newly acquired businesses with lower initial margins and navigated supply chain cost pressures. The recovery to 11.0% in 2024 reflects operational integration maturing, contract mix shifting toward higher-margin MSA work, and management's disciplined focus on capital reallocation toward Energy and Utilities segments. EBITDA margins remain thin at 6-9% — characteristic of specialty contractors — but FCF conversion improved dramatically in 2024 with record operating cash flow of $508M.

Cash Flow Quality — 2024 as the Inflection Point. The most significant financial signal in this analysis is the FCF trajectory. After near-breakeven FCF in 2022 (driven by working capital consumption from rapid revenue growth and acquisitions), Primoris generated an estimated $378M in free cash flow in 2024. This reflects the maturation of acquired businesses, disciplined working capital management, and lower interest expense as debt was repaid. FCF conversion (FCF/Net Income) improved to approximately 2.1x in 2024 — indicating earnings quality is strong and not dependent on accounting choices.

Capital Allocation — Disciplined Pivot to Organic Growth. CapEx has grown modestly from ~$80M to $130M over five years — capital-light relative to revenue scale — reflecting the services-heavy business model. Debt reduction has been prioritised post-acquisition, with management signalling a net cash positive balance sheet by end of 2025. The dividend has grown from $0.06 to $0.08 per quarter — a 33% increase — signalling management confidence in sustained cash generation. Going forward, capital allocation will focus on higher-return Energy and Utilities organic growth rather than transformational M&A.

The base case assumes 10% annual revenue growth driven by backlog conversion (1.9x cover), continued Energy segment strength, and stable utility spending. At current market price (~$128), Primoris trades at a meaningful premium to its 5-year historical average on both EV/EBITDA and P/E — reflecting the market's recognition of the infrastructure supercycle tailwind and Primoris's strong backlog position. On a Price/FCF basis, the valuation is more reasonable given the step-change improvement in cash conversion in 2024. Our DCF analysis at a 9% WACC and 3% terminal growth rate implies a base case intrinsic value of $118–142 per share, suggesting the stock is approximately fairly valued with modest upside in the base case and significant upside in the bull scenario. The primary re-rating catalyst would be sustained EBITDA margin expansion above 8% — which would justify the current premium multiple.

Section 08
FP&A Analyst — Executive Takeaway
For the CFO / Board of Directors

Primoris Services has executed a textbook infrastructure platform buildout — scaling from $3.5B to $6.4B in revenue over four years through disciplined M&A, organic growth in high-tailwind end markets, and progressive operational integration. The record $508M operating cash flow in FY2024 confirms that earnings quality has arrived after an integration-heavy 2022–2023 period. With $11.9B in backlog providing nearly two years of revenue visibility and a net cash positive balance sheet anticipated in 2025, the fundamental financial position is the strongest in the company's recent history. However, margin expansion from the current 6.9% EBITDA level to 8–9% remains the central value-creation challenge — and the primary metric this board should hold management accountable to over the next 24 months.

01
Margin Expansion Programme
Drive EBITDA margin from 6.9% toward 8.5% through MSA mix improvement, Energy segment prioritisation, and integration cost reduction. Every 100bps = ~$64M additional EBITDA at current revenue.
02
Backlog Quality Over Quantity
With $11.9B backlog, focus should shift from backlog growth to backlog margin — ensuring new contract wins meet minimum margin thresholds and MSA percentage continues to grow.
03
Capital Return Acceleration
Net cash positive balance sheet in 2025 creates capacity for dividend growth and share repurchases — both EPS-accretive and positive signals to capital markets on management confidence.
