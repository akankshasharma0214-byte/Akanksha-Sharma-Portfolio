Akanksha Sharma
FP&A Analyst · MS Financial Analytics, Stevens Institute of Technology

Microsoft Corporation is the world's second-largest company by market capitalisation, operating as a diversified technology platform across three segments: Intelligent Cloud (Azure, server products), Productivity and Business Processes (Microsoft 365, LinkedIn, Dynamics), and More Personal Computing (Windows, Xbox, Surface, Search). Under CEO Satya Nadella's leadership since 2014, Microsoft has executed one of the most successful enterprise transformations in corporate history — pivoting from a software licensing model to a cloud-first, AI-first subscription and consumption platform.

Revenue is generated through a highly recurring model: subscription contracts (M365, Azure committed spend), consumption-based billing (Azure pay-as-you-go), gaming revenue (accelerated by the $69B Activision Blizzard acquisition in 2023), and advertising (Bing, LinkedIn). This diversification across multiple high-growth verticals, combined with extraordinary operating leverage, has produced operating margins exceeding 45% — among the highest of any large-cap technology company globally.

For FP&A analysts, Microsoft is the benchmark study for subscription economics, cloud segment reporting, operating leverage analysis, and AI monetisation — directly relevant to understanding how Amazon Web Services, Google Cloud, and other hyperscaler finance functions manage revenue, cost, and capital allocation decisions.
