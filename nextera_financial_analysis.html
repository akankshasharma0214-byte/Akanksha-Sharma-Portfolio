Section 03
Trend Analysis

Adjusted EPS Growth — The Most Consistent Growth Story in Utilities. NextEra has compounded adjusted EPS at 8–13% annually for six consecutive years — a record of consistency that is virtually unmatched in the utility sector. From $2.40 in FY2020 to $3.71 in FY2025, adjusted EPS has grown 55% over five years — driven by FPL's regulated capital growth (~10% regulatory capital employed expansion annually) and NEER's growing contracted renewable portfolio. Management has guided 8%+ annual adjusted EPS CAGR through FY2027, and has beaten or matched the top end of guidance in multiple consecutive years. This track record of delivery is the core reason NextEra commands a premium valuation vs utility peers.

Revenue Volatility — Commodity-Driven Distortion. Total revenue appears volatile — from $17B to $28B in FY2023, then back to $24.8B in FY2024. This is almost entirely driven by commodity price movements in NEER's energy trading and power contracts, not underlying business changes. Adjusted EPS — which strips out mark-to-market and commodity impacts — is the correct metric for evaluating underlying earnings power. The FY2023 revenue peak at $28.1B reflected elevated power prices; the subsequent normalisation does not reflect any business deterioration.

CapEx Intensity — The Growth Engine and the Cash Flow Challenge. FPL's capital expenditure program — growing from ~$6.5B in FY2020 to ~$9.8B in FY2025 — is the primary driver of regulated earnings growth. Each dollar of CapEx earns a regulated return of approximately 11.6% ROE, making this a highly predictable earnings growth engine. The consequence is significant negative free cash flow — NextEra consistently spends more in CapEx than it generates in operating cash flow. This is funded through a combination of equity issuance (NextEra Partners), project financing, and access to investment-grade debt markets at competitive rates. The business model fundamentally requires external capital access — making interest rate movements and capital market conditions critical to execution.

Backlog Inflection — AI/Data Center Demand Accelerating NEER Growth. The most significant recent development is the backlog stepping from 20 GW (FY2023) to 30 GW (FY2025) — a 50% increase in two years. This reflects both continued strong renewable demand from utilities and an entirely new demand source: technology companies requiring large-scale clean energy for data centers and AI compute infrastructure. Google Cloud's strategic partnership with NextEra, announced in FY2025, is emblematic of this trend. Hyperscalers are signing 10–20 year PPAs for multi-gigawatt clean energy projects — providing NEER with long-term contracted revenue visibility that further de-risks the growth story.

At approximately $91 per share, NextEra trades at 23x forward adjusted earnings — a meaningful premium to traditional utility peers (16–18x) that reflects the company's superior growth profile. Our DCF analysis using 7.5% WACC and 3.5% terminal growth rate (higher than utilities norm due to NEER's reinvestment runway) produces an intrinsic value range of $85–105 per share — suggesting modest upside in the base case with more significant upside (~40–50%) if the bull case materialises. The valuation debate for NextEra centres on one question: is a 23x P/E justified for a company growing EPS at 8–9%? At a PEG ratio of approximately 2.5x, the stock is not cheap by traditional standards. However, the combination of regulated utility defensiveness (FPL), contracted renewables growth (NEER), 10% annual dividend growth, and the emerging AI/data center catalyst creates a genuinely differentiated investment profile vs both pure utilities and pure clean energy companies.

Section 08
FP&A Analyst — Executive Takeaway
For the CFO / Board of Directors

NextEra Energy is the world's leading clean energy infrastructure platform — uniquely positioned at the intersection of regulated utility stability (FPL) and high-growth renewable development (NEER). The company has delivered 8%+ annual adjusted EPS growth for six consecutive years and is now entering what may be the most favourable demand environment in its history: AI infrastructure requirements are creating a new category of power demand that is structurally aligned with NextEra's wind, solar, storage, and nuclear capabilities. The 30 GW renewables backlog — record by a wide margin — reflects this demand inflection and provides extraordinary forward earnings visibility. The Board's primary financial governance focus should be on capital allocation quality: ensuring that each GW of backlog converts to in-service capacity on time and on budget, and that project-level returns on the 30 GW pipeline remain above the cost of capital across various interest rate scenarios. NextEra's business model requires continuous external capital access — the FPL rate agreement and NEER's contracted PPA portfolio de-risk this access — but any deterioration in capital market conditions or project execution discipline would compound quickly across a $30B+ annual CapEx programme.
